BIRDWATCHING HELSINKI

Best of Birding in Finland's capital

TOURS

Book a birdwatching experience in Helsinki with me and discover the nature that surrounds us!

Easily accessible from the heart of the city, the best local nature reserves have a lot to offer to the keen birder. Woods, reedbeds, fields and mudflats form a myriad of microhabitats that host countless birds. From the rare Citrine Wagtail to the majestic White-tailed Eagle, from the endearing Horned Grebe to the hectic Common Rosefinch, there are wonders waiting to be discovered!

The main hotspots are Vanhakaupunki, Laajalahti and Suomenoja. They all have their own specificities, but one thing for sure is that they have birds, and they are easy to explore through paths, boardwalks and birdwatching towers.

The places

Vanhakaupunki: an extensive wetland, complete with light woods, fields, reedbeds and ponds. Citrine Wagtail is the real prize there, but there's also an abundance of waders, ducks, warblers and other songsters. Highlights include a White-tailed Eagle nest among a busy cormorant colony and maybe a Penduline Tit.
Laajalahti: an amazing wetland on the other side of the city, with a similar habitat patchwork. Spectacular during migrations for ducks, terns and waders, we will also look for a Common Rosefinch singing it's signature "Pleased to meet you" song. The woods hold Wood Warblers and woodpeckers, among others.
Suomenoja: this wetland holds the largest Black-headed Gull colony in Finland. Among those loud birds, we'll find nesting Horned Grebe, Common Goldeneye, Eurasian Shoveler... it's a small but wild place, there is always something happening. A bay nearby may hold Red-necked Grebes.

All seasons

In Helsinki, the peak period for birds is from early May to late June, when birds are migrating north, singing, nesting, raising chicks... but all seasons have something to offer. March and April bring the first migrating birds (Northern Lapwing, grey geese, Whooper Swan...) and some waders already show up in late July for autumn migration, which continues through until October. Winter is a quiet time, but birding in the forest and around feeders can yield jewels such as White-backed Woodpecker, Eurasian Bullfinch or even a Northern Hawk-Owl or a Great Grey Owl!
